When calculating a pet's food intake, it is important to know their current weight and body condition score. Body condition score is commonly measured on either a 5- or a 9-point scale. A body condition score is useful because there could be two cats that both weigh 15 lb, but only one cat may be considered obese according to their body condition score! A similar logic follows for our canine companions.

For this calculator, we will be using a scale of 1-9.

{% if species == "Canine" %}

BCS 1

You can easily see (on short-haired dogs) the ribs, spine, and bones of the pelvis from a distance. You can also easily feel all of them. You don't feel any fat on the ribs or spine. Significant muscle loss can be felt and/or seen (on short-haired dogs) over bony areas such as the spine, ribs, head, and thigh bones.

BCS 2

You can easily see (on short-haired dogs) and feel the ribs, spine, and bones of the pelvis. You don't feel any fat on the ribs. Some muscle loss can be felt and/or seen (on short-haired dogs) over bony areas such as the spine, ribs, head, and thigh bones.

BCS 3

You can easily see (on short-haired dogs) and feel the ribs. The tips of the spine and bones of the pelvis are prominent. You don't feel any fat on the ribs or spine. There is a waist with a belly tuck.

BCS 4

You can easily see (on short-haired dogs) and feel the ribs. They have a bit of fat covering them. There is a waist with a belly tuck. The waist is easily seen when looking down from above your dog.

BCS 5

You can easily see (on short-haired dogs) and feel the ribs. They have a bit of fat covering them. There is a waist with a belly tuck.

BCS 6

You can feel the ribs and they have some fat covering them. There is a belly tuck and a small waist when viewed from above.

BCS 7

You can barely feel the ribs under a thick layer of fat. There is also fat over the spine and where the spine meets the tail. The belly tuck and waist may be absent or barely visible.

BCS 8

You can barely feel the ribs under a thick layer of fat, or you may need some pressure in order to feel the ribs.There is also significant fat over the spine and where the spine meets the tail. There is no belly tuck or waist. The belly may look distended.

BCS 9

You cannot feel the ribs under a thick layer of fat. There is also significant fat over the spine and where the spine meets the tail, neck, and legs. There is no belly tuck or waist. The belly is distended.

{% elif species == "Feline" %}

BCS 1

You can easily see (on short-haired cats) the ribs, spine, and bones of the pelvis from a distance. You can also easily feel all of them. You don't feel any fat on the ribs or spine. Severe belly tuck.

BCS 2

You can easily see the ribs and spine on short-haired cats. Minimal muscle mass over the spine. You don't feel any fat on the ribs or spine. Pronounced belly tuck.

BCS 3

You can easily feel the ribs. They have a bit of fat covering them. You can see the spine on short-haired cats. There is an obvious waist behind the ribs. There is minimal belly fat.

BCS 4

You can feel the ribs. They have a bit of fat covering them. There is a noticable waist behind the ribs with a slight belly tuck. Abdominal fat pad absent.

BCS 5

Well-proportioned. You can see a waist behind the ribs. You can feel the ribs. They have a slight bit of fat covering them. Abdominal fat pad minimal.

BCS 6

The ribs can be felt with a slight excess of fat covering them. The waist and abdominal fat pad are distinguishable but not obvious. Abdominal tuck absent.

BCS 7

You can barely feel the ribs under a moderate layer of fat. The waist is poorly discernable, and there is an obvious rounding of the belly. Moderate abdominal fat pad.

BCS 8

Ribs cannot be felt due to an excess of fat. Absent waist and obvious round belly with a prominent abdominal fat pad. Fat deposits are seen over the back.

BCS 9

Ribs cannot be felt due to a heavy layer of fat covering them. Distention of the belly with no waist and extensive abdominal fat deposits. Heavy fat deposits are seen over the back, face and limbs.
